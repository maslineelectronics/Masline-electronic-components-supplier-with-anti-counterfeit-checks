The electronic components industry faces increasing risks from counterfeit and substandard parts. These fake components can cause critical system failures, production delays, and even safety hazards. Masline recognizes the impact of these risks and has implemented a robust anti-counterfeit program to protect its customers.

### Common Counterfeit Issues
- **Tampered Labels:** Misleading part numbers and logos.  
- **Re-marked Components:** Used chips re-labeled as new.  
- **Non-conforming Materials:** Parts that fail functional testing.  
- **Recycled Packaging:** Unsanitary or altered shipping materials.  

Masline combats these problems through comprehensive supplier validation and advanced testing technologies that guarantee authenticity and performance.

## Masline’s Anti-Counterfeit Process

Masline employs a multi-tiered approach to eliminate counterfeit risks from its supply chain. This process ensures that each component is genuine, compliant, and traceable to its original manufacturer.

### Step-by-Step Verification
1. **Supplier Approval:**  
   Masline sources only from authorized and verified suppliers who meet international quality standards.

2. **Visual and Microscopic Examination:**  
   Each component undergoes detailed inspection for label accuracy, surface markings, and packaging consistency.

3. **X-Ray and XRF Testing:**  
   Advanced imaging and material analysis confirm structural and elemental accuracy.

4. **Electrical Function Testing:**  
   Sample parts are tested to confirm they meet specified electrical characteristics.

5. **Traceability Documentation:**  
   Lot codes, date codes, and Certificates of Conformance are reviewed and attached to every shipment.

Through these steps, Masline maintains a zero-tolerance policy for counterfeit components in its supply chain.
